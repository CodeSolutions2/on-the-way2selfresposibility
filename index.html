Observation 0: Disaster and Data



Disaster occurs when people, objects, and/or things are transformed in an unproductive manner. The dictionary definition of "destroy" is "to reduce (an object) to useless fragments, a useless form, or remains, as by rending, burning, or dissolving; injure beyond repair or renewal; demolish; ruin; annihilate" [1]. Destruction can take the form of many behaviors, including: physical destruction of life (ie: violence), physical destruction of material property (ie: breaking a PC because you did not win at a video game), destruction of mental health (ie: excessive competition, harassment, self-sabotage).

When one lives a life influenced by disaster and destruction it means that:

  1. one can not control their brain in a self-productive manner, such that they can think logically and calmly without being triggered by strong emotions.
  2. one acts as if they do not want a future by: creating no short and long-term goals, having no self-improvement, not acquiring objects (ie: money, car, home) and/or titles of value and significance. Accomplishments, created by daily actions, show that one has a future in our society. It is very difficult to create nothing and/or destroy everything, and suddenly start over again and catch-up to the current level of modern society; it takes decades or more to build stable structures. Destroying one's things or having no ambition to accomplish something creates no future.
  3. one desire no self-improvement for themselves or others. Figuratively speaking, one desires nothing except "dirt and ash", which is all that is left after significant disaster and destruction events. Another parallel of destruction of property, is "returning to nature or a simplistic way of living" in an advanced technological age. It is not logical or productive for the evolution of human civilization to return to using simplistic methods 100 years ago, after having advanced scientific understanding of our environment and ourselves. When a person graduates from university, do they return to elementary school to learn how to read? One does not, one in fact values and improves upon what they learned before, accumulating all of the knowledge.
  4. one lives in the past because everyone who does not destroy their things are constantly creating, working together, and advancing to the future; unfortunately leaving behind people who destroy themselves and others around them. Feelings of revenge and competition are often fostered by those who were "left behind", unwillingly causing themselves to repeat an endless cycle of destructive behavior.
  5. one becomes less connected to reality due to fear and lack of interest in building a productive future and life; they become isolated and have less access to modern technology.
  6. one creates limited mental beliefs of what they can and can not do because they do not understand the full range of possible choices that are available. In short many people whom experience disaster/destruction in their life lack education about the activities that they are involved in, poor life decisions are often made without up-to-date knowledge about societal related activities and career.

Creation and destruction are similar to the Birth and Death cycle in numerical modeling, people who destroy are likely to die-out due to lack and those who create are likely to survive due to growth and improvement. I have observed that many people who have had disaster or tremendous loss in their life, have anger and resentment for their losses, making violence and destruction justified. Therefore, just by being exposed to unfortunate situations, it is human nature to repeat destruction in some form (ie: vandalism, abuse, harassment). People exposed to disaster may influence people to destroy things, simply due to conditioned behavior that everyone deems as normal.

Not only is disaster a physical phenomenon, it has lasting physical and psychological effects. Physical and psychological effects include depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), gastro-intestinal disorders [2]. By understanding which features/factors are involved with disaster, perhaps individual change and self-empowerment can be obtained by simple day-to-day behaviors related to reversing disaster exposure behavior.

Observation 1: Self-evolution through transformation



We all have unfortunate experiences in our lives, it is a choice to transform these experiences into something positive or “run away” from them. “Running away” could include avoidance, distraction from the unfortunate situation by over focusing on something else, or asking someone else to solve the situation for you. Transforming the situation implies understanding the situation (context, cause and effect), and then taking logical steps to change the outcome in a controlled manner. The act of transforming unfortunate experiences into positive experiences consists of three steps:

  1. try to understand the problem using cause-and-effect thinking of actions that create responses,
  2. be able to control and direct one’s emotional state despite the current situation,
  3. long-term monitoring of cause-and-effect thinking of actions that create responses, and synthesizing key information that create better life-situations.

Step 1 is a Data Science principle, called finding key features to understand system function, thus allowing one to predict and control future outcomes. This Data Science principle can be used in the context of daily-life, such that one needs to find key actions (ie: features) to understand their life (ie: system function) thus allowing one to predict and control future life outcomes. Successful and happy people have learned which actions cause certain life-outcomes, and they repeatedly choose specific actions to create or control desired future goals. Successful and happy people are considered in society as people who "know themselves", because they understand their "Data Science system function" or action-response relationships in their life. Learn about the your daily-life problem/s by testing an action and observing a response. Actions can be as simple as taking a different way at work or learning a new hobby. After performing these "test actions", observe the effects in your life; keep a log listing the action and its corresponding effect in your life with respect to the problem/s. By understanding the cause and effect relationship with one’s problems, one can slowly improve and positively transform one’s life by identifying and controlling key actions to obtain desired outcomes.

Understanding cause-and-effect observations of action-response takes time because action-response data needs to be collected (ie: logging action-response outcomes) and patterns need to be understood, therefore do not be discouraged that your life will not positively changed over-night. Thus, as mentioned in Step 2, maintaining a hopeful and neutral emotional state is key to obtaining one's desired life goals, because it enables logical observation of action-response patterns and good decision making. In addition to being hopeful and not influenced by strong emotions, it is important to be self-motivated and focused on one's daily objectives. In order to maintain a self-motivated state, it is recommended that one constantly focus upon at least one positive realistic long-term goal. Having a continuous long-term objective in mind allows one to maintain focus on their goal instead of being distracted, thus increasing the probability of achieving the goal. What one thinks about constantly is generally what one does, says, and who one is. By staying hopeful, emotionally calm, and focusing on objectives, you will not have time nor even think about being discouraged about your life not yet being in a desired state. You will feel that your life is incrementally advancing just by maintaining these mental objectives.

Finally in Step 3, it is important to adopt a long-term action-response strategy for solving daily-life problems to make long-lasting change. Short-term help from others may improve a problem temporarily but the problem is likely to reoccur because action-response patterns that caused the original problem were not changed. Many people have problems that orignate from themselves because we perform actions that create certain responses, one’s experiences and perception of the world creates the opportunities in one’s life.

In Data Science, a long-term system monitoring strategy is called Machine Learning Operations (MLOps) or Developement Operations (DevOps). Machine Learning is a scientific field where data-driven predictive models provide users with probablistic results of certain outcomes. MLOps monitors long-term changes in model prediction based on new data, and retrains the model when baseline data trends change such that users can have accurate predictions based on their data [1]. Similarly, DevOps is the process of making a software system available to many users and long-term changes are monitored in order to give targeted feedback to users of the system [2]. Continually synthesizing the observations of action-response such that key actions/features/factors for one’s problems are identified, is important to change the outcome of the situation with learned feedback, and thus have a more stable life (ie: a more stable model for MLOps, a more stable system for DevOps).

Transition to the future! As you understand the underlying action-response patterns in your life, move away from unsuccessful patterns, and adopt more life-fulfilling patterns, new ideas, life desires, and long-term goals are likely to arise. Also, be aware that your thoughts, personality/character, and/or perspective may evolve too. As a result of character and perspective change due to self transformation, ideas and concepts that were exciting and funny before, may be less exciting and funny because a new view-point and understanding of the consequences with respect to the old view-point were realized. For example, laughing at someone falling due to miscoordination may have been funny before understanding that falling down could happen to anyone and it is a common event that is not surprising or exciting, it is a painful event that requires prevention; once these simple ideas are realized laughing is not interesting and other responses are adopted.

In the Data Science Prompt below, make a schedule to evolve your life from one life-path to another desired life-path. By having a daily schedule of what objectives are required to obtain a desired life, short-term and long-term planning to make stable lasting change is manageable.

Observation 2: Ironic Importance of debit



Debit, or lack of something, can be applied to any concept and it is a type of feedback/reponse of a situation based on actions. Debit, lack of something, makes one realize the available choices that they can make. Debit is an noun that has a connotation associated with money and finance, but debit could be applied to any subject such as opportunity or creativity.

“Opportunity debit”, scarcity of opportunities, is a common phenomenon that happens to everyone. It generally means that one is not surrounding themselves with people that have the same expectations and knowledge as them; there is misalignment of intention and interest. The best way to solve opportunity debit is to participate in events and activities that align with the desired expectations of the opportunity. For example, if one wants to be a Data Scientist, participate in Data Science blogs, classes, group analysis sites like Kaggle, online conferences, bootcamps if you are lucky, and anything related to the subject of Data Science. The more one participates with the topic of the opportunity that is desired, the more likely the opportunity is possible. One can use the expression ‘Fake it until you make it’ because one is performing the actions and intentions to obtain the skills for the desired opportunity, until they obtain the opportunity and then embody the thing/s that they desired to be. The least effective way to solve “opportunity debit” is to focus on the disaster, hate of lack, and negative emotions for not being accepted into the activities that are desired in life. People who have already been accepted into activities/jobs that you want to participate in are actively focused on the activity and a corresponding lifestyle with the activity. It is not effective to “show up to the door” of a group of people who you want to join in a negative, hateful, and angry state because you have not yet been accepted or previously rejected from the group. Therefore, focus on the objectives of your goals and the activities that you want to partiipate in, and do not focus on strong emotions. As mentioned, in Observation 1, "What one thinks about constantly is generally what one does, says, and who one is.". Stay hopeful, emotionally calm, and focus on your objectives such that you can show others your true personality. Focusing on strong emotions distracts people from focusing on the current activity (ie: job, etc). Carrying around hateful negative emotions makes one: not lead a normal life, have limited social and financial opportunities, not have a future to look forward to living, not have long-term happiness.

Similar to "opportunity debit", "creativity debit" is considered to be a new problem of the 21st century. “Creativity debit” is the lack of original ideas to create new processes and products that are necessary for innovation and societal advancement. There is controversy about being creative because it has been shown that increased creativity is correlated with increased dishonesty or honesty, because creative individuals maybe able to realize more strategies of solving a problem [1]. Nevertheless, creativity is a character attribute that is desirable, like opportunity, because it provides more benefits than disadvantages to both individuals and society. In Data Science, many ideas and processes are recycled ideas from existing professional fields. Data Science champions the behavior of not having “creativity debit”. A simple way to add creativity to one’s life is to incorporate ideas from a different data source/activity. Taking a different route from work, eating at a new restaurant, or learning a new hobby are all simple ways to be more creative. Data Science incorporates ideas from many different data sources, while maintaining the core goal of quantifying real-life and giving real-time feedback. Thanks to the fact that Data Science/numerical computing is pervasive in all fields of study/data sources, all fields are able to advance their domain knowledge faster and thus contribute in the closed-loop process of creativity.

In addition, to understanding that debit is just a type of feedback/response to actions and thus can be controlled, it is important to realize that debit is every present and it is not bad. Debit/Lack is an upward concial/helix spiral, small diameter at the bottom and large diameter at the top. As one takes responsibility to improve their life they receive less lack in improved areas, thus signifying competence and mastry of the topic/s. A natural process of life is that as one becomes competent in certain topics, more challenging and advanced topics appear and thus lack in these new areas/topics need to be managed or learned. The metaphor of "spiraling out in life", means that "the more one knows, the more they realize how much they do not know" because they are exposed to more and more advanced information as they continue to learn, and have a global perspective of the body of knowledge [2].

Mismanagement or poor ability to learn these new areas, results in another cycle of problems and lack. Therefore, it is constructive to constantly be aware of new progress and current information, such that optimal decision making can be made to solve the new problems with minimal failure. Therefore, debit is important and for a reason, it means that one tried or is trying to be responsible in improving themselves and others around them. Failure caused by debit means that one was not able to make the correct choices due to lack of knowledge and/or ineffiecient action-response feedback management.

Having a debit implies that a risk was taken, it is rare to make zero failures. Risk is the possibility of not achieving something despite making efforts to accomplish it. There are many different types of risks: obtaining an education and/or credentials to become socially and economically established in society, creating knowledge and/or products for financial gain, improving/positively transforming one’s self and/or others around them. To obtain an education/degree with a remaining student loan debit means that a risk was taken where optimal financial outcomes were not possible, however work was accomplished and personal advancement was obtained.

Responsibility and self-transformation are coupled with debit because change can not be made without risk taking. Positive change is difficult to implement and almost impossible to achieve without failure of some sort, one can always be better at everything they accomplish. It is normal to have debit and unachieved consequences for: life advancement, planning endeavors, and entrepreneurial endeavors. What debit/lack do you have? And how can you minimize the debit by performing action-response observations, identifying key actions, and correcting responses with improved actions? Happy debit reduction!

Observation 3: Your problems are your Secret Gifts



Many people ask other people to help them with their life, directly and indirectly. However, when does asking for help render one too dependent on others, where they do not take personal responsibility for their proper life? Many people do not realize that they are helped all the time with their life, simply by the fact that they exist and participate in life activities like getting an education. All the problems in life are indirect ways to have help, help created by one's “own hands”. Most people “run away” from the problems in their life and give them to other people instead of embarrassing them and unraveling them such that they can find the “secret gifts”.

If you can not find a job, it is a “secret gift” to tell you that you are doing something wrong at that time. Wrong does not mean good or bad, is just means that there is a conflict. One needs to follow the action-response observation steps to understand what they should do correctly, in order to obtain the things that they need and want in life. Brainstorming, reading/watching many sources of information, learning new things, and practicing everyday is necessary to find the “secret gifts” in one’s problems.

I personally do not like to write about myself, but I can not find a job for years despite having a solid education in a Data Science related-field (numerical analysis and control theory), and many short-term internships and postdoctorates using Data Science modeling algorithms and methods. As someone who strongly values education, I immediately thought that the problem was my lack of education in the field so I started taking training courses, participating in any online activities related to Data Science, I read Data Science blogs and even started my own Data Science code blog, and much more. With every new activity/choice that I completed with respect to improving my Data Science profile, a new advanced topic/door that could take me to the next level of understanding Data Science appeared on the blogs or by Internet search. Despite the fact that I still can not find a job even though I apply to jobs weekly, I believe that I am one of the best versions of myself; applying for a job yourself is a form of asking for help in a respectable manner of accepted service. If I had “ran away”, complained, or became depressed about the situation I would have a lot less in my life, with or without a job. Those other alternate versions of myself would have been less responsible and less proud of the things that I had accomplished before having trouble finding a job. I believe that by taking responsibility for my situation, valuing the things that I have, and constantly trying to improve the things in my life I will have a chance for future success.

Another key aspect of deciphering "secret gifts" from your problems is focusing on your own things present in your life, instead of enviously wishing that you had the accomplishments and things that peers, friends, and family have. The fact that one has peers, friends, and family that are successful just means that you have the potential to accomplish similar things with your own things and opportunities in your own life. If you are surrounded by successful people, it is statistically likely that you will be successful too simply by mimicking successful behavior (ie: proven action-response observations that produce the desired responses). Social media sites like LinkedIn and Facebook measure one’s potential for success based on the success of their peers, friends, and family [1]. When peers, friends, or family accomplish success the marketing of the social media site often automatically contacts their social network to notify others that they could have more potential for success too. Peers, friends, and family rush to the person who gained success and ask to be their "friend" and are motivated to copy what they did in order to gain more success too. This form of marketing biases successful people to have more friends, and less successful people to have less friends. It encourages a range of emotions and behaviors exchanged between less and more successful people, including: aggressiveness, competitive comparison between one's self and others, strong focus on the successful person’s life, jealousy, strong negative or positive judgment (putting people in low-status groups/"metaphorical homelessness in the street" or on pedestals/"metaphorical high chairs/kings/queens/gods"), hatred, envy, desperate copying without thinking about what is best for one's self. In this marketing scheme, very little focus is put on self-reflection and self-motivation to fulfill one's own potential and life-path without strongly looking at the lives/accomplishments of peers, friends, and family.

One’s lack of success with using their opportunities does give them the liberty to take the things and opportunities of more successful peers, friends, and family by unconsciously wishing for their lack of success. One should use the success of others to motivate themselves to utilize their own resources more efficiently such that they can equally obtain success too. If you have university peers that are Data Scientist, but you are having a hard time becoming a confirmed Data Scientist, it just means that you have the potential to be a Data Scientist and you need to use your own resources and past experiences more efficiently. For example, having a lack of knowledge in a fundamental topic for your profile maybe more important to repair than learning the newest Data Science techniques that your peer just learned. One could use more time to ensure that fundamental topics are known, and less time keeping up-to-date with new techniques until all fundamental topics have been reviewed. After topics have been revisted, reviewing fundamental topics using condensed notes is important to keep the ideas in memory. Having a strong solid base about how things function allows for idea generation, acceptance, participation, and thus success. As I continue to advance my Data Science career, I constantly need to choose between 2 or 3 topics to study and acquire into my skill-set at the same time. Often the topics that were fundamentally connected to my education and training, were the skills that allowed me to feel accepted and have a sense of high competence for my profile. Being able to offer ideas that are fundamentally solid or reinterpreted with one's education and experience can make one feel accepted, competent, and an active member of their career/life.

Unraveling the “secret gifts” from your life problem's is performing the correct actions for your life path to advance yourself. Peer success provides a proven measure that you can accomplish the same success, and it gives guidance for what activities to engage in. The experience of doing the same activity (learning, reflection) will be different for each person because each person is different but the competence outcome will be similar. In the Data Science Prompt exercise 1, input a list of job titles of your peers. The LLM will order the job titles from most to least successful in terms of importance. Next, in exercise 2, input the most successful job of your peer's and your career domain. The LLM will tell you what is the equivalent career title of the successful peer job in your career path. Then visit Observation 1 Exercise 2, insert your current job title and the successful job title in your career domain, to make a concrete schedule on how to level-up your career in your field. Be inspired by the success of your peers and use your own interests to create a happy successful life!
